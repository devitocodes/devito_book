Exact solution of a damped 1D wave equation

Mathematical model:

$$ \begin{equation} \frac{\partial^2 u}{\partial t^2} + b\frac{\partial u}{\partial t} = c^2\frac{\partial^2 u}{\partial x^2} + f(x,t), \label{wave:pde3} \end{equation} $$ \( b \geq 0 \) is a prescribed damping coefficient.

Ansatz:

$$ u(x,t) = e^{-\beta t} \sin kx \left( A\cos\omega t + B\sin\omega t\right) $$

Boundary condition: \( u=0 \) for \( x=0,L \). Fulfilled for \( x=0 \). Requirement at \( x=L \) gives

$$ kL = m\pi,$$ for an arbitrary integer \( m \). Hence, \( k=m\pi/L \).

Inserting the ansatz in the PDE and dividing by \( e^{-\beta t}\sin kx \) results in

$$ -\omega^2(A\cos\omega t + B\sin\omega t) -b\beta (A\cos\omega t + B\sin\omega t) +b\omega (-A\sin\omega t + B\cos\omega t) = -(A\cos\omega t + B\sin\omega t)k^2c^2 $$

Each of the sine and cosine terms in time must balance:

$$ \begin{align*} -\omega^2A -b\beta A -b\omega B &= -Ak^2c^2\\ -\omega^2B -b\beta B -b\omega A &= -Bk^2c^2 \end{align*} $$ Multiply the first by \( B \) and the second by \( A \):

$$ \begin{align*} -\omega^2AB -b\beta AB -b\omega B^2 &= -ABk^2c^2\\ -\omega^2AB -b\beta AB -b\omega A^2 &= -ABk^2c^2 \end{align*} $$ Subtracting gives \( A^2=B^2 \).

$$ omega^2 + b\omega + b\beta + k^2c^2 = 0 $$ Seems that we can choose \( \beta \) freely, but that must be wrong. Must have \( \beta = b \)... Compare with damped_wave_equation.pdf lecture note from the net.
